Privacy & Cookies

This page explains what kind of information is collected when you use Genectics.com, how it is used, and which third parties may process data on our behalf.

The site is operated by ronarn AS, a Norwegian company that runs educational and comparison websites in health, genetics and personal finance.

1. What we do not collect

  • We do not run user accounts or logins on Genectics.com.
  • We do not ask you to upload health records or genetic data.
  • We do not store copies of your DNA reports or raw DNA files.
  • We do not sell personally identifiable information to third parties.

Any personal information you choose to share with DNA companies or merchants we link to is handled under their own privacy policies, not ours.

2. Analytics & usage data

Like most websites, Genectics.com uses privacy-friendly analytics to understand how visitors use the site and to improve our content. This may include:

  • Pages visited and time spent on each page
  • Device type (desktop, tablet, mobile) and anonymised technical details
  • Approximate location at country or region level
  • Referring sources (for example, search engines or other websites)

Analytics data is used in aggregate form to see which guides and comparison tables are useful and where visitors struggle to find what they need.

3. Cookies and similar technologies

Genectics.com may use cookies or similar technologies for:

  • Basic site functionality and security
  • Anonymous audience measurement (analytics)
  • Attribution of affiliate referrals to partners

Affiliate networks and merchants (for example Amazon or Living DNA) may also set their own cookies when you click through to their websites. These cookies are used to track which publisher referred a sale so that commissions can be calculated correctly.

You can control or delete cookies at any time using your browser settings.

4. Affiliate links and third-party sites

Genectics.com contains links to third-party websites that sell DNA kits and related products. When you click these links, you leave our site and are subject to the privacy and cookie policies of the destination site.

We do not control and are not responsible for how those companies handle your data. Before buying or creating an account with any DNA provider, you should carefully read their current privacy policy, terms of service and data retention rules.

For more on how affiliate links work on this site, see our Affiliate Disclosure.

5. Log files and security

Our hosting environment may automatically log certain technical information when you visit Genectics.com, such as:

  • IP address (which may be treated as personal data in some regions)
  • Browser type and version
  • Date, time and pages requested
  • Referring URLs and error codes

These logs are primarily used for security, troubleshooting and to prevent abuse of the service. They are automatically rotated and retained only for as long as necessary for these purposes.

6. Contact by email

If you contact us by email (for example at info@genectics.com), we will receive whatever information you choose to include in your message, such as your name, email address and the content of your inquiry.

We use this information only to respond to your request and to maintain a record of important correspondence. We do not add you to mailing lists or sell this information.

7. Legal basis & your choices

Depending on where you live, our use of analytics, security logs and affiliate tracking may be based on legitimate interest, consent or a mix of both under applicable privacy laws (such as GDPR in the EU/EEA).

You can usually:

  • Adjust your browser to block or delete non-essential cookies
  • Use browser extensions or settings to reduce tracking across sites
  • Contact us if you have questions about how data is used on Genectics.com

8. Changes to this policy

We may update this privacy and cookie information from time to time, for example when we add new features or integrate with new analytics or affiliate partners. The “last updated” date at the bottom of this page tells you when the text was most recently revised.
