MyHeritage DNA: best overall starting point

If you want one kit that balances price, ethnicity detail and family matching, MyHeritage DNA is our default recommendation.

  • Very solid ethnicity estimates, especially for European ancestry.
  • Strong matching for Scandinavia, Eastern Europe, the Balkans and Jewish heritage.
  • Clean interface and easy family tree tools.
  • Frequently discounted on Amazon.
Our verdict: the best “first DNA kit” for most Amazon buyers. Buy MyHeritage DNA on Amazon

AncestryDNA: best for big family trees

AncestryDNA is built around one thing: relatives. If you care more about cousin matches and records than tiny percentage tweaks, this is the kit designed for you.

  • Access to one of the largest consumer DNA databases.
  • Excellent match system for building out huge family trees.
  • Tight integration with Ancestry’s historical records (subscription optional).
Our verdict: ideal if “family tree” is your main hobby. Buy AncestryDNA on Amazon

FamilyTreeDNA: for serious DNA hobbyists

FamilyTreeDNA is less “pretty”, more powerful. It’s what you choose when you already know the basics and want deeper tools and segment analysis.

  • Advanced tools for analysing matches and shared segments.
  • Good upgrade if you’ve tested elsewhere and want extra depth.
  • Interface is more technical, but very capable once you get used to it.
Our verdict: the right upgrade when you’ve outgrown beginner kits. Buy FamilyTreeDNA on Amazon

LifeDNA: health & lifestyle over ancestry

LifeDNA turns the focus away from “where are my ancestors from?” and towards “how might my genetics affect lifestyle and wellness decisions?”.

  • Reports centred on wellness, nutrition and lifestyle.
  • Interesting if you already know your ancestry and want something different.
  • Not ideal if your main goal is building a family tree.
Our verdict: niche but useful for health-focused users. Buy LifeDNA on Amazon

What about LivingDNA and 23andMe?

Two honourable mentions that are not the core of this Amazon-focused list:

  • LivingDNA – still one of the best options for extremely detailed British/European ancestry and a strong privacy stance. Great if you prioritise European detail and data protection.
  • 23andMe – a leader in traits and health-oriented reports, but less genealogy-focused than MyHeritage DNA and AncestryDNA.
