7 Things You Must Know Before Buying a DNA Kit

Privacy concerns have become the #1 topic for DNA testing — and for good reason. When you test with LivingDNA, MyHeritage, AncestryDNA, or 23andMe, you aren’t just buying a product; you’re sharing the most personal data you possess.

Below are the 7 most important things to understand in 2025 — written to help you choose the safest company, not the biggest one.

1. Who Owns Your DNA? (Short Answer: You Do — But Not Equally)

All companies say you own your DNA. But what they can legally do with it varies dramatically.

  • LivingDNA: Best privacy. No pharma partnerships, no data selling, no law-enforcement cooperation, and strict UK privacy standards.
  • MyHeritage: Strong privacy. No police access and Israel/EU-based privacy protections.
  • 23andMe: Lowest privacy rating. Large pharma partnerships and revenue from anonymized genetic licensing.
  • AncestryDNA: Does not sell data, but may cooperate with law enforcement with a court order.
If privacy matters most, LivingDNA and MyHeritage are usually the safest choices.

3. Cybersecurity: Who Protects Your Data Best?

  • LivingDNA: Smaller database → less attractive to hackers. Strong UK cybersecurity rules.
  • MyHeritage: Good security; previous breach was email-only, not DNA.
  • AncestryDNA & 23andMe: Largest databases → biggest targets, which increases the potential impact of a breach.

4. What Happens to Your DNA Sample?

LivingDNA destroys samples physically faster than others. MyHeritage destroys samples on request. 23andMe and AncestryDNA typically store samples unless you explicitly ask them to destroy them.

5. What Results Affect Family Members?

LivingDNA produces the least controversial relative matches since its database is smaller and privacy-focused. AncestryDNA and MyHeritage produce more matches, which may reveal surprise relatives, unknown parents, or unexpected heritage.

6. Are Health Reports Accurate?

23andMe remains the strongest for health-related reports. LivingDNA focuses mainly on ethnicity. MyHeritage Health exists, but is more limited. AncestryDNA has discontinued health reports entirely.

7. Hidden Consent Settings You MUST Review

All companies have toggles for things like research, pharma partnerships, DNA matching, sample storage, and third-party data use. The safest default settings tend to be with LivingDNA. The least privacy-friendly defaults are usually found at 23andMe.

Final Recommendation (Trust & Safety) If your priority is privacy, accurate ethnicity, no data selling and zero police access, the kit with the strongest consumer protection for most users is: LivingDNA — our safest DNA kit pick for 2025
