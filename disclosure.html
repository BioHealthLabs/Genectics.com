Affiliate Disclosure

Genectics.com is an independent comparison site focused on at-home DNA test kits for ancestry, family matching and health & wellness. To keep the site free to use, we sometimes earn money when you buy products through links on our pages.

This page explains how that works, who we work with, and how we protect editorial independence when affiliate links are involved.

1. How this site makes money

Some links on Genectics.com are affiliate links. If you click one of these links and then buy a product, we may receive a small commission from the merchant at no extra cost to you.

The main affiliate relationships currently used on Genectics.com include, but are not limited to:

  • Living DNA via the CJ affiliate network
  • DNA test kits and related products sold on Amazon
  • Other DNA and health kit merchants via affiliate networks such as CJ, Impact and Awin

These commissions help cover hosting, maintenance and editorial work without charging visitors a subscription fee.

2. No paid rankings or sponsored reviews

Our comparisons and descriptions are created using publicly available information, documentation from providers and our own editorial framework. We do not sell rankings or “featured placement” to DNA companies.

  • Providers cannot pay to be included in a table.
  • Providers cannot pay to be ranked higher than competitors.
  • Providers cannot pay to change our editorial notes or star ratings.

If a brand offers us special marketing materials, pre-written copy or paid placements, we either decline or clearly label such content outside of the comparison framework.

3. How we label affiliate links

Where practical, affiliate links are clearly labelled or placed in obvious “View offer”, “Buy kit” or “Visit provider” buttons. On some pages we may also explicitly mark affiliate relationships in text, for example: “We may earn a commission if you buy via this link.”

If a section or table is heavily driven by affiliate-supported products, this is explained in the surrounding text so you understand the commercial context.

4. Impact on ratings and recommendations

We always try to align our editorial priorities with what is useful for readers: clarity, value, and realistic expectations about what a kit can and cannot do. Affiliate commissions do not override this.

  • Star ratings are relative and based on category performance, not payouts.
  • We may list competing products that pay little or no commission if they make sense for certain users.
  • We may choose to highlight a kit with a lower commission if we believe it is a better fit for a common use case.

If a conflict of interest is unavoidable (for example, an affiliate partner is also a major player in a category), we aim to surface that context directly in the text.

5. Relationship to the broader publisher network

Genectics.com is part of a wider network of educational and comparison sites published by ronarn AS. The overarching governance, ethics and independence standards are described on: Organization.bio , Transparency.bio , and Disclosure.bio .

Those pages explain how affiliate relationships are handled across the entire network and how conflicts of interest are managed at publisher level.
