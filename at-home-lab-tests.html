At-home lab tests explained: what they measure — and what they don’t

At-home lab tests are often confused with DNA tests. They are fundamentally different tools, designed to measure current biomarkers, not genetic code.

Short version: DNA tests look at what you were born with. Lab tests look at what is happening in your body right now.

What is an at-home lab test?

An at-home lab test typically involves collecting a small blood, saliva, stool or swab sample at home and mailing it to a certified laboratory for analysis.

The results focus on biomarkers such as hormones, cholesterol, vitamin levels or infections — not ancestry or genetic traits.

How at-home lab tests differ from DNA tests

Aspect DNA Tests At-Home Lab Tests
What is measured Your genetic code Blood, hormones, nutrients, infections
Changes over time No Yes
Main use Ancestry, traits, long-term risk Current health status
Medical diagnosis No No (screening only)

When an at-home lab test can make sense

  • You want to check vitamin or mineral levels
  • You are monitoring hormones or cholesterol
  • You want discreet STI screening
  • You prefer starting with a screening before visiting a clinic

Important limitations & safety notes
