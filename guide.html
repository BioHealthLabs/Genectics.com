7 Things You Must Know Before Buying a DNA Kit

Privacy concerns have become the #1 topic for DNA testing — and for good reason. When you test with LivingDNA, MyHeritage, AncestryDNA, or 23andMe, you aren’t just buying a product; you’re sharing the most personal data you possess.

Below are the 7 most important things to understand in 2025 — written to help you choose the safest company, not the biggest one.

1. Who Owns Your DNA? (Short Answer: You Do — But Not Equally)

All companies say you own your DNA. But what they can legally do with it varies dramatically.

  • LivingDNA: Best privacy. No pharma partnerships, no data selling, no law-enforcement cooperation, and strict UK privacy standards.
  • MyHeritage: Strong privacy. No police access and Israel/EU-based privacy protections.
  • 23andMe: Lowest privacy rating. Large pharma partnerships and revenue from anonymized genetic licensing.
  • AncestryDNA: Does not sell data, but may cooperate with law enforcement with a court order.
If privacy matters most: choose LivingDNA or MyHeritage.

3. Cybersecurity: Who Protects Your Data Best?

  • LivingDNA: Smaller database → less attractive to hackers. Strong UK cybersecurity rules.
  • MyHeritage: Good security; previous breach was email-only, not DNA.
  • AncestryDNA & 23andMe: Largest databases → biggest targets. Costs: higher risk of mass-scale exposure.

4. What Happens to Your DNA Sample?

LivingDNA destroys physically faster than others. MyHeritage destroys on request. 23andMe and AncestryDNA store samples unless asked to destroy them.

5. What Results Affect Family Members?

LivingDNA produces the least controversial relative matches since its database is smaller and privacy-focused. AncestryDNA & MyHeritage produce more matches, which may reveal surprise relatives, unknown parents, or unexpected heritage.

6. Are Health Reports Accurate?

23andMe remains the leader. LivingDNA focuses on ethnicity. MyHeritage Health exists, but is limited. AncestryDNA discontinued health reports.

7. Hidden Consent Settings You MUST Review

All companies have hidden toggles for: research, pharma partnerships, DNA matching, sample storage, and third-party data use. The safest defaults: LivingDNA. The least safe defaults: 23andMe.

Final Recommendation (Trust & Safety)
If your priority is Privacy, Accurate ethnicity, No data selling, and Zero police access, then the DNA test with the strongest consumer protection is: LivingDNA — The safest DNA kit in 2025
