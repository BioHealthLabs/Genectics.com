Affiliate Disclosure

Genectics.com is an independent comparison site. To keep the site running, we sometimes use affiliate links. This means that if you click a link to a DNA test or other product and make a purchase, we may earn a commission – at no extra cost to you.

We know that trust is everything in this niche. We therefore separate how we make money from what we recommend as clearly as we can.

How affiliate links work

On Genectics.com you may see:

  • Buttons and text links that go to Amazon or other merchants.
  • Links marked as “affiliate”, “sponsored” or similar in context.
  • Occasional tracking parameters in the URL so the merchant knows the sale came from us.

If you buy something through those links, the merchant pays us a small commission. You pay the same price as if you had gone there directly.

Networks and programs we use

Genectics.com may be enrolled in affiliate programs such as:

  • Amazon Associates
  • CJ Affiliate (formerly Commission Junction)
  • Other reputable affiliate networks and direct partner programs

The exact mix of programs can change over time as we test which partnerships work best.

Editorial independence

Commissions influence how we monetise the site – not what we say about products. Our principles:

  • We aim to describe strengths and weaknesses of each kit in plain language.
  • We do not sell ranking positions to brands.
  • Affiliate partners do not get to edit or approve our comparison content.

Why we disclose this

U.S. Federal Trade Commission (FTC) guidelines and similar regulations in other countries require websites to be transparent about financial relationships that might influence content. We support this. You deserve to know when a link can result in a commission.

If you have questions about how we make money or think something on the site is unclear, you can always reach out via our contact page.
